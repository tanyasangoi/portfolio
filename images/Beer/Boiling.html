Boiling & Hopping

The temperature needs to be higher!

In the third step, the wort collected from mashing has to be boiled. The wort is boiled for about an hour while hops and other spices are added several times.

Hops are the small, green cone-like fruit of a vine plant. Initially used as preservatives, they provide bitterness to balance out all the sugar in the wort and provide flavor. Before you can start boiling, add bittering hops here.

Drag the temperature on the thermometer up to around 212F to start boiling. Boiling is generally done for a little more than an hour.

Flavoring and aroma hops add a crisp hoppy flavor and aroma to the wort. They are generally added towards the end of the boiling process.
Add flavor hops and aroma hops to your boil.
