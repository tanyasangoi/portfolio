There are two main types of beers: ales and lagers. Depending on the type of beer you want to brew, select one of the yeasts below.

Ale yeast generally ferments at the top.

Ales are fermented at around room temperature. Set the temperature to 60-68F on the thermometer.

Ales are fermented at room temperature for 30 days.

Notice the bubbles of carbon dioxide forming as yeast breaks down sugars in the wort to form alcohol.

Lager yeast generally ferments at the bottom.

Lagers are fermented at colder temperatures than ales. Set the temperature to around 50F on the thermometer.

Lagers are fermented at these cold temperatures for a few months.

Notice the bubbles of carbon dioxide forming as yeast breaks down sugars in the wort to form alcohol.
