As a UX Design Intern at Wally, I designed solutions to complex problems in the IoT (Internet of Things) space.
My main project involved redesigning a dashboard for the newly designed enterprise product named Wally Pro.
This dashboard would allow a user to monitor thousands of sensors and troubleshoot any problems that came up.
In addition, I also worked on redesigning a mobile application that assisted users with product installation.

Meet my team!
Given that Wally was a startup company, I reported directly to the principal UX designer, who was the only other designer on the team.
The rest of the team consisted of app and web developers, cloud and network engineers, sales and product managers.

The Wally product suite consists of:
1) Smart sensors which can detect environmental changes such as a water leak.
2) The Wally Hub which controls all the sensors and the water shutoff valve
3) The Shutoff Valve which can switch off the water flow in case of a leak
4) Mobile and desktop apps which help the user monitor these products and related activity.

Here is an example of a whiteboard exercise.
To solve a problem, I would first list down all the available information followed by a list of questions that I had for either the product managers or the engineering team.
Since Wally followed the agile software development process; I would participate in the daily engineering stand-ups and scrum meetings so that engineering and design worked cohesively.

This a sketch of one of the product dashboards.
Once I had all the information at hand, I would start sketching low fidelity prototypes. Then I would ask for feedback from my manager and engineering team before designing the high fidelity prototypes. Once these designs were approved, I was responsible for generating the specifications required for web development.
